Statistics Model Results over 2014

These statistics show a methodically robust backtest of the results over 2014.

Below you will find FootballMetrics' results of 2014. The computer-generated probabilities have been backtested extensively and we show you how the model performed on the 2014 season for the English Premier League, the Spanish La Liga and the German Bundesliga. All results are based on average odds which are obtained minutes before the start of a match. While it is certainly possible to find more favourable odds, we do not want to overestimate our results using unrealistically high odds. Nevertheless, if you manage to find higher odds, you could earn an even larger profit.

Profit Trend

Information

This graph shows the models' profit trend over 2014. It shows the profits of a fixed stake betting strategy, in which 10 units are staked on each bet.

Every data point is a week of bets, where the first week starts at January 1 and week 37 ends on December 29. Weeks in which no football was played are not shown. The red line displays the mean of the profits per week. A larger average dispersion between this mean line and the trend line is undesirable since this will mean large up and down swings in profits.

On the second tab, the profit trend per month is displayed.

Information

Weekly Profit

On the right the weekly profits in units of 10 and in return on investment (ROI) over 2014 are shown.

The graph shows that a profit has not been achieved in every week. However the majority of weeks were positive and together they more than outweigh the negative weeks.

Every league on its own has a positive average return on investment. Nevertherless, it is inevitable that each league will face a negative week from time to time. By wagering your money on multiple leagues the number of total negative weeks shrinks, since bad luck in one league can be offset by good luck in another. Once we add more leagues to the model, it's expected that the number of negative weeks is reduced even more!

Profit Share per League

Information

This graph shows the distribution of profits over different football leagues. The total wagered units and ROI for each league appear when you click on the specific league. Spreading your total wagered amount over a larger number of games reduces the variance of your returns, hence creating a more stable stream of profits.

We have incorporated multiple leagues in the model to be able to select the most profitable bets and still bet on a large number of games. As mentioned before, we are working hard to implement more leagues. Please let us know which are your favorite leagues to be added to the system!
